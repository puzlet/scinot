Scientific Notation 2

Powers of ten are a core idea of scientific notation.

The key is to understand what the top number does. (It's called the exponent)

For positive exponents, picture repeated multiplication.

103 means multiply by 10 three times.

For negative exponents, picture repeated division: 10-3 means divide by 10 three times.

Another point of view is to shift the decimal point.

An exponent of -3 means, starting from 1. , move the decimal point three places to the left.

The ideas above are often enough.

For example, 104×10-3 is the same as muliplying by 10 4 times and dividing by ten three times. Overall, equal to multipling by 10 once. 104×10-3=101
